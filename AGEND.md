# Xtra Fresh Cakes Website Agenda

## Purpose
This document translates the PRD into an execution agenda for designing and building the Xtra Fresh Cakes website. The site is intended to launch first in Lagos, focus on ceremony cakes, support quote-based ordering, and position the brand against leading Nigerian cake vendors that already emphasize premium presentation, delivery convenience, and occasion-based shopping.[cite:46][cite:48][cite:47]

## Product direction
Xtra Fresh Cakes should launch as a premium Lagos-first cake brand website with a luxury visual identity, a mobile-first quote workflow, and subtle pastry visibility rather than a broad bakery catalog.[cite:46][cite:47][cite:54] The core strategic benchmark is the combination of premium positioning, delivery confidence, and online ordering clarity seen across Cakes and Cream, Baqers, and Diva Cakes.[cite:46][cite:48][cite:47]

## Project objectives
- Build a premium website that makes a new brand look credible, established, and competitive with top Nigerian cake vendors.[cite:46][cite:48][cite:54]
- Generate quote requests and confirmed orders for ceremony cakes in Lagos through a friction-light mobile experience.[cite:47][cite:48]
- Communicate 24-hour delivery availability for eligible Lagos orders with clear expectations and operational trust signals.[cite:48][cite:49]
- Prepare the platform for transfer-first payments now and Paystack-based automation later.[cite:61][cite:66][cite:70]
- Create strong visibility foundations through structured content, product-like service pages, and search-friendly markup.[cite:31][cite:32]

## Audience focus
### Primary users
- Lagos customers ordering for birthdays, weddings, anniversaries, engagements, children’s celebrations, and corporate ceremonies.[cite:47][cite:46]
- Busy buyers who need delivery confidence and want to complete requests quickly from mobile devices.[cite:48][cite:72]
- Premium buyers who care about design quality, presentation, and trust before making payment.[cite:46][cite:54]

### Core user needs
- See premium cake options by ceremony type.
- Request a quote without confusion.
- Understand delivery coverage, timing, and payment steps quickly.
- Trust the brand enough to pay in full by transfer for a custom order.[cite:48][cite:61][cite:72]

## Brand and UX agenda
### Brand direction
- Position Xtra Fresh Cakes as a premium ceremony cake house.
- Use white and deep red as the brand anchors, supported by soft neutrals so the site feels luxurious rather than loud.
- Build trust through elegant typography, editorial-style layouts, and high-quality photography once assets are available.

### Experience principles
- Mobile-first and conversion-focused.
- Quote-led rather than cart-led for most ceremony products.
- Fast, guided, and reassuring at every step.
- Clear support pathways through WhatsApp and direct contact.
- Minimal friction before inquiry, maximum clarity after inquiry.[cite:36][cite:39][cite:72]

## Feature agenda
### 1. Homepage
Build a premium landing experience that introduces the brand, shows ceremony categories, explains the ordering path, displays signature cake designs, and highlights Lagos delivery confidence.[cite:46][cite:48] The homepage should also include trust indicators, testimonials, WhatsApp support access, and a clear path into the quote flow.[cite:48][cite:72]

### 2. Occasion-led browsing
Organize discovery by ceremony type because leading competitors already frame shopping around occasions rather than generic bakery categories.[cite:46][cite:47] Each category page should feature inspiration, example pricing guidance where appropriate, lead times, and strong quote CTAs.[cite:47]

### 3. Quote workflow
Create a structured quote request flow capturing event type, date, Lagos location, servings, budget range, flavors, design direction, urgency, add-ons, and reference uploads. This flow is the core conversion engine because the business has chosen a quote-based model rather than fixed-price checkout for most cakes.

### 4. Payment workflow
Support full-payment confirmation after quote approval, starting with bank transfer and later upgrading to Paystack-driven automation.[cite:61][cite:66][cite:70] The payment journey should include a branded instruction page, proof-of-payment or manual confirmation handling, and a clear order-confirmed state.[cite:61][cite:63]

### 5. Delivery information
Add a dedicated Lagos delivery page that explains eligible 24-hour delivery, cutoff expectations, service zones, urgent order policy, pickup option if offered, and third-party/in-house dispatch logic.[cite:48][cite:49] This page should reduce buyer uncertainty and help qualify orders earlier in the journey.

### 6. Trust and credibility
Include testimonials, customer celebration photos, policy clarity, support response expectations, and premium packaging or freshness messaging. Because Xtra Fresh Cakes is a new brand, the website must create confidence through presentation and transparency before scale-based social proof exists.[cite:48][cite:54][cite:72]

### 7. Search visibility
Structure the site so each major ceremony page can rank independently, and apply structured data on relevant pages because Google documents product and ecommerce structured data as beneficial for search understanding and rich result eligibility.[cite:31][cite:32] FAQ and breadcrumb markup should also be considered where appropriate.[cite:32]

## Content agenda
### Content to create
- Brand story and positioning copy.
- Luxury homepage messaging.
- Category-page copy for each ceremony type.
- Quote-flow labels, helper text, and confirmation messaging.
- Delivery, refund, cancellation, and payment policy content.
- FAQ content for ordering, delivery, customization, and transfers.
- WhatsApp script templates for fast customer follow-up.

### Visual assets needed
- Logo and brand system.
- Premium cake photography by category.
- Packaging and delivery imagery.
- Close-up texture shots.
- Celebration lifestyle shots.
- Optional subtle pastry visuals so pastries remain secondary, not dominant.

## Design agenda
### Visual system
- White-led background system with deep red accents and warm luxury neutrals.
- Strong typography pairing with premium editorial feel.
- Elegant cards, restrained color usage, and refined spacing.
- Distinct visual treatment for wedding and high-value cake sections.

### UI priorities
- Sticky mobile CTA for quote requests.
- Strong visual hierarchy on hero and category pages.
- Clean forms with short progressive steps.
- Fast-contact modules for urgent orders.
- Light and dark mode only if it supports the luxury look cleanly; otherwise prioritize a polished light-first experience during scope planning.

## Technical agenda
### Recommended build priorities
- CMS-capable site structure for content updates.
- Quote form with admin-manageable submissions.
- Media management for gallery growth.
- SEO metadata and structured data support.[cite:31][cite:32]
- Analytics integration for traffic and conversion tracking.
- Payment architecture that starts manually and can later connect to Paystack channels.[cite:66][cite:70]

### Core integrations
- WhatsApp click-to-chat.
- Email notifications for quote submission.
- Optional Google Maps or location cues for Lagos delivery trust.
- Future Paystack integration for transfer/card automation.[cite:66][cite:70]

## Admin and operations agenda
### Internal workflow support
- View and manage quote requests.
- Update quote status: new, reviewing, quoted, awaiting payment, confirmed, in production, dispatched, delivered.
- Assign delivery method: in-house or third-party.
- Confirm transfer payments.
- Manage cake portfolio and testimonial content.

### Business rules to define before build
- Minimum order value.
- Rush-order surcharge.
- Delivery fee by Lagos zone.
- Quote response SLA.
- Cutoff rules for 24-hour delivery.
- Redesign or revision limits after quote acceptance.

## SEO and growth agenda
- Create dedicated ceremony landing pages with strong intent-focused copy.[cite:46][cite:47]
- Add structured data for service/product-like pages, organization details, breadcrumbs, and FAQs where suitable.[cite:31][cite:32]
- Build a gallery and inspiration engine that can later support blog or seasonal campaign pages.
- Plan future city-expansion templates for national growth after Lagos validation.

## Accessibility and quality agenda
- Use semantic HTML, strong color contrast, accessible forms, visible focus states, and keyboard-friendly interactions in line with WCAG 2.2 guidance.[cite:37][cite:43]
- Make mobile interactions primary, with clear buttons, short forms, and visible support actions.[cite:72]
- Keep page performance high through optimized imagery, lightweight interactions, and careful loading strategy.[cite:31][cite:32]
